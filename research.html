Research

Bridging quantum computing, chemistry, and machine learning

Research Overview

My research sits at the intersection of quantum computing and quantum chemistry, with a focus on making molecular simulation practical on near-term hardware. Working in the group of Prof. Rahul Maitra at IIT Bombay, I develop resource-efficient quantum algorithms for electronic structure. Pre-fault-tolerant quantum devices — known as NISQ machines — are limited by noise and short coherence times, which means the algorithms we run must be exceptionally resource-efficient.

To address this, I develop methods that leverage Generative AI and machine learning — particularly Restricted Boltzmann Machines (RBM) — to intelligently construct quantum circuits (ansatzes) that encode the electronic structure of molecules with minimal gate depth. I also work on the Projective Quantum Eigensolver (PQE) framework and dynamic adaptive ansatz construction strategies, incorporating many-body perturbation theory for operator screening. My work has accumulated 112 citations. The goal is to reduce quantum resource requirements without sacrificing accuracy — enabling meaningful quantum advantage on today's hardware.

Research Themes

Projective Quantum Eigensolver (PQE) & VQE Frameworks

Designing the Projective Quantum Eigensolver (PQE) and variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) variants — including adaptive ansatz construction — that accurately compute ground-state molecular energies. My work introduces Zero Noise Extrapolation (ZNE-PQE) and adiabatically decoupled subsystem evolution strategies to push accuracy on real hardware, while supervised machine learning aids dimensionality reduction in the parameter optimization landscape.

Generative AI for Ansatz Construction

Using Restricted Boltzmann Machines (RBM) and other generative machine learning models — trained on the physics of electron correlation — to propose chemistry-inspired ansatz circuits. Rather than fixed templates, these dynamic ansatzes adapt to the molecule, predicting dominant high-rank determinants from low-rank training data. Bayesian hyperparameter optimization ensures efficient performance with minimal training overhead.

NISQ-Era Resource Efficiency

Many-body perturbation theoretical measures and supervised machine learning methods to screen and discard low-impact quantum gates before circuit execution. This dramatically reduces gate count and qubit requirements, making quantum chemistry calculations tractable on current hardware generations. Methods include the COMPASS protocol for operator commutativity prescreening and neural error mitigation techniques compatible with shallow ansatz circuits.

Ongoing Projects

Adaptive Generative Models for Multi-Reference Systems

Extending the generative AI ansatz framework to strongly correlated, multi-reference molecular systems — a notoriously hard problem for both classical and quantum approaches. Current work explores how generative models can be conditioned on multi-configurational reference states.

Noise-Aware Circuit Optimization

Developing hardware-aware compilation strategies that tailor circuit structure to the specific noise profile of a target quantum device, integrating seamlessly with the dynamic ansatz generation pipeline.

Benchmarking on Real Quantum Hardware

Running and validating developed algorithms on IBM Quantum devices via Qiskit, including experiments on IBM Heron R2 and R3 quantum processors at scales up to 52 qubits. These large-scale demonstrations characterize the gap between ideal simulation and real-hardware performance, feeding results back into algorithm and circuit design for quantum-centric supercomputing.
